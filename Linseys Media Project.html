Quote:

"Of course, some breakups are worse than others. It’s generally harder to be rejected dented you the rejecting, and anyone who mopes and dwells on what they’ve lost and how lousy they feel during a break up is likely to have a hard time; rumination prolongs our distress, whereas reflection-- seeking meaning in her experiences and looking to learn from them – is associated with positive adjustment in recovery." Explination:

This song shows that transition between sadness to recovery. In the beginning, she talks about being afraid of being without him. Then after repeated nights of pondering all his negative actions she gained the strength to move on. When she used to think about him she got more and more depressed but soon once she reflected on their relationship she could move on.

Quote:

"Second, commited partners think of each other with positive illusions, idealizing each other and perceiving their relationship in the best possible light. The partner’s faults are judged to be relatively trivial, relationships deficiencies are considered to be relatively unimportant, and a partner’s behavior is dismissed as an unintentional or temporary aberration." (Miller, 2015, p.431)

Explination:

He starts off the song point out her flaw of a ‘smart mouth,’ but it doesn’t bother him. He’s willing to look past it because he loves her. The chorus emphasizes how he loves all parts of her and not just the good things. He appreciates all of those things as well and wants her to be her true self just like he is his true self.
