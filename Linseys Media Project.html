We usually assume that our messages have the impact that we intended, but we rarely know that they do. More often than we realize (Keysar & Henly, 2002), we face an interpersonal gap in which the sender's intentions differ from the effect on the receiver. (p. 142)

Explination:

This song is a story about two people who have different interpretations of the word bitch and how it causes conflict in the future. The young man recalls his mother singing a song and referencing herself as a ‘bad bitch.’ Since his mother referenced herself as a ‘bad bitch’ he takes it to mean more a less a strong woman. On the other hand, a young girl watched a music video where the rapper mentions ‘bad bitches’ and had number of provocative women surrounding him so she took the term ‘bad bitches’ to mean be provocative. When the boy and girl meet in the future they have a conflict of understanding because they have different views on the meaning of ‘bad bitch.’

Accommodating people tolerate destructive behavior from their partners without fighting back; they swallow insults, sarcasm, or selfishness without retaliating. By so doing, they avoid quarrels and help dispel, rather than perpetuate, their partners' bad moods. That's usually good for the relationship. Such behavior may involve considerable self- restraint, but it is not motivated by weakness; instead, accommodation often involves a conscious effort to protect the partnership from harm.
